Vampire power or standby power is electricity that is being wasted by electrical devices when they are turned off but are still plugged in. Lawrence Berkeley Labs estimates the amount of vampire power as 10% of a U.S. households annual electrical bill. This can average 1300 kWh of electricity per year. With tiered electrical rates as high as $.34/kWh in California, this can cost a homeowner over $440 per year in unnecessary electrical costs. Electrical rates are rising at an average of 5% per year, so these costs will only get higher in the future. The majority of this vampire power is consumed by only a few home appliances such as TV's, set-top boxes, stereos and PC's. A focused attack on these biggest offending appliances can yield a large payoff with a minimum investment.
